Smart tips
🛒
Build meals around zero-point foods
Start every meal with a zero-point base - eggs, fish, chicken, or vegetables - then add smaller amounts of higher-point ingredients. This stretches your budget further.
🍬
Added sugar is the biggest point driver
Added sugar adds points faster than almost anything else. Check labels carefully - sauces, dressings, breads, and yogurts often have hidden added sugars.
💪
Protein is your best friend
High-protein foods significantly lower points. Choose lean meats, eggs, fish, Greek yogurt, and beans to keep points low and stay full longer.
🚶
Walk your way to bonus points
Hitting 7,000+ steps a day earns you approximately 2 extra points. That is 14 extra points over a whole week!
⚖️
Your budget changes as you lose weight
As your weight drops your daily budget will gradually decrease. Recalculate your budget in the My Budget tab every 10-15 lbs lost.
🥑
Healthy fats lower your points
Unsaturated fats from avocado, nuts, olive oil, and salmon actually reduce your point total. Don't fear healthy fats - they work in your favor on this plan.
📅
Use weekly flex points wisely
You get around 28 weekly flex points for treats, dining out, or special occasions. Unused flex points don't roll over but having them prevents feeling deprived.
🍞
Best breads for WW
Rye sourdough, whole grain, and sprouted grain breads are your best choices - higher fiber and protein, lower added sugar. Avoid white bread and sweet rolls.

Always verify point values with the official WW app. Zero-point food lists may vary for diabetic or menopause plans.
